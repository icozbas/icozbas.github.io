THE ENGINEER
BEHIND THE SYSTEM.

> CONTEXT: Intersection of Design & Operational Reality

A Systems Engineering perspective positioned at the intersection of system-level design and operational reality in defense and aerospace.

Background: A foundation built on Mechanical Engineering (system dynamics and failure mechanisms) and Industrial Engineering (optimization and decision-making under constraints).

In large-scale military aviation programs, a recurring structural problem exists: Traditional Integrated Logistics Support (ILS) frameworks are largely static, while modern fleets operate in dynamic and uncertain environments.

The Focus: Engineering decision-centric sustainment systems that turn logistics data into auditable readiness decisions. Aligning with the industry transition from ILS to Integrated Product Support (IPS) to enable proactive support strategies.
